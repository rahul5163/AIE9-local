# 30-Day Exercise Plan - Morning Energy Focus

## Week 1 (Days 1-7) - Foundation Building
- **Monday** (Cardio & Flexibility)
  - 10 min brisk walk or jog
  - 10 min stretching (hamstrings, quadriceps, shoulders)
  - 5 min deep breathing exercises

- **Wednesday** (Strength & Energy Boost)
  - 5 min warm-up (marching in place)
  - 15 min bodyweight strength: squats, push-ups, lunges (2 sets of 8-12 reps)
  - 5 min balance exercises (single-leg stands)

- **Friday** (Active Recovery)
  - 30 min yoga or gentle stretching

## Week 2 (Days 8-14) - Building Intensity
- **Monday** (Increased Cardio & Flexibility)
  - 12 min brisk walk or jog
  - 12 min stretching (include hip flexors)
  - 6 min deep breathing exercises

- **Wednesday** (Increased Strength)
  - 5 min warm-up
  - 18 min bodyweight exercises (3 sets of 8-12 reps)
  - 7 min core exercises (planks, bird dogs)

- **Friday** (Rest Day Activity)
  - 20-30 min walk or leisurely bike ride

## Week 3 (Days 15-21) - Progressive Challenge
- **Monday** (Cardio Focus)
  - 15 min interval walking (1 min fast, 2 min slow)
  - 10 min dynamic stretching
  - 5 min mindfulness breathing

- **Wednesday** (Strength Focus)
  - 5 min warm-up
  - 20 min circuit training (3 sets, max reps)
  - 5 min balance & mobility

- **Friday** (Active Recovery)
  - 30 min restorative yoga or Pilates

## Week 4 (Days 22-28) - Peak Performance
- **Monday** (Endurance Focus)
  - 20 min brisk walk or jog
  - 5 min full-body stretch
  - 5 min deep breathing and mindfulness

- **Wednesday** (Strength Endurance)
  - 5 min warm-up
  - 25 min strength circuit (increased reps/weights)
  - 5 min flexibility work

- **Friday** (Celebration Activity)
  - Choose a favorite activity (swimming, dancing, cycling)

## Special Focus: Combat Morning Tiredness
- Prioritize hydration upon waking
- Light stretching before bed
- Consistent sleep schedule
- Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep nightly

## Modifications by Fitness Level
- **Beginner**: Reduce duration/intensity, focus on form
- **Intermediate**: Add weights, increase reps gradually
- **Advanced**: Incorporate plyometrics, resistance bands
