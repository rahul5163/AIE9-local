# Alex's 2-Week Vegetarian Nutrition Plan
## Improving Diet Quality with Plant-Based Focus

### Program Overview
- **Duration:** 14 days of structured meal planning
- **Diet Type:** Vegetarian (no meat, fish, or poultry)
- **Focus:** Balanced nutrition, adequate protein, variety, and convenience
- **Meal Structure:** Breakfast, lunch, dinner, and healthy snacks

## Vegetarian Protein Sources Guide

### High-Protein Foods to Include Daily:
- **Legumes:** Beans, lentils, chickpeas, peas
- **Nuts & Seeds:** Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, hemp seeds
- **Dairy:** Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, milk, cheese
- **Eggs:** Whole eggs, egg whites
- **Plant Proteins:** Tofu, tempeh, seitan
- **Grains:** Quinoa, oats, whole wheat

### Protein Content Reference:
- 1 cup cooked lentils: 18g protein
- 1 cup cooked quinoa: 8g protein
- 1 cup Greek yogurt: 15-20g protein
- 1 large egg: 6g protein
- 3 oz tofu: 9g protein
- 2 tbsp almond butter: 7g protein

## Meal Prep Tips

### Sunday Prep (1-2 hours):
1. **Cook grains in bulk:** Prepare quinoa, brown rice, and oats for the week
2. **Prep proteins:** Cook lentils, bake tofu, hard-boil eggs
3. **Wash and chop vegetables:** Store in clear containers
4. **Make overnight oats:** Prepare 2-3 servings
5. **Prep snack portions:** Portion nuts, cut vegetables

### Daily Habits:
- Drink water first thing in the morning
- Include a protein source at each meal
- Fill half your plate with vegetables
- Keep healthy snacks visible and accessible

---

## Hydration & Supplement Recommendations

### Daily Hydration:
- **Target:** 8-10 cups of water daily
- **Options:** Plain water, herbal teas, infused water with fruits
- **Timing:** Glass of water upon waking, before meals, and throughout the day

### Recommended Supplements for Vegetarians:
- **Vitamin B12:** 2.4 mcg daily (essential for vegetarians)
- **Omega-3:** Algae-based supplement (1-2g daily)
- **Iron:** If needed based on blood work (take with vitamin C)
- **Vitamin D:** Especially during winter months

### Natural Nutrient Boosters:
- **Iron absorption:** Combine iron-rich foods with vitamin C (citrus, bell peppers)
- **Calcium:** Include tahini, leafy greens, fortified plant milks
- **Zinc:** Pumpkin seeds, cashews, chickpeas

---

## Success Tips

### For Busy Days:
- Keep emergency snacks: nuts, fruit, protein bars
- Use frozen vegetables for quick stir-fries
- Pre-made salads last 2-3 days with dressing on side
- Batch cook grains and legumes on weekends

### Eating Out Guidelines:
- Look for Mediterranean, Indian, or Mexican restaurants
- Ask about vegetarian options or modifications
- Don't be afraid to request substitutions
- Focus on dishes with legumes, grains, and vegetables

### Progress Tracking:
- Note energy levels throughout the day
- Monitor how you feel after meals
- Track any digestive changes
- Take photos of colorful plates for motivation

Remember: The goal is progress, not perfection. Focus on adding nutritious foods rather than restricting, and celebrate the variety and flavors in plant-based eating!
